## 1. Legal structure — start simple

You said no LLC, and that's a fine starting point. Here's the honest comparison:

| Structure | Setup | Liability | Taxes | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Sole proprietor** | Free (just start) | Unlimited personal liability | Pass-through to your 1040 | <$50K/yr revenue, low risk |
| **Single-member LLC** | ~$50–500 to file (state-dependent) | Limited — separates personal assets from business | Pass-through by default | >$50K/yr OR any risk of being sued |
| **S-Corp election** | LLC + IRS election | Same as LLC | Saves self-employment tax at higher revenue | >$80–100K profit |

**My recommendation:**
- **Start as sole proprietor** while you validate (first 3–6 months).
- **Form an LLC** the moment:
  - You sign your first paying client, OR
  - Revenue hits ~$25K, OR
  - You're touching client data / credentials (you will be — AI agents log conversations)
- **File an S-Corp election** once profit clears ~$80K/yr (saves ~15% on self-employment tax).

State-by-state LLC filing cost (2026):
- Cheap states: KY ($40), MS ($50), AR ($50)
- Mid: TX ($300), FL ($125), AZ ($50)
- Expensive: CA ($70 + $800 annual min tax), MA ($500), NY ($200 + publication)

A lot of people use a Delaware LLC + a registered agent (~$150/yr) for credibility even if they're elsewhere. Skip this until you're profitable.

**Insurance to consider:**
- **General liability** — protects you if you accidentally break something on a client's site (~$500/yr).
- **Professional liability (E&O)** — protects you if your code causes them a financial loss (~$800/yr).
- **Cyber liability** — protects you if client data leaks. Worth it once you handle customer PII (~$1,000/yr).

## 2. Pricing — why these numbers

Your three tiers ($497 / $997 / $2,497) are calibrated for **home-services trades** specifically:

### Why these buyers say yes
- A plumber doing $1M/yr with 3 trucks loses $30–80K/year in missed calls. They pay $497/mo without blinking.
- An HVAC company paying $5K/mo to Google Ads can save $2K/mo switching to better-converting site + agent. $997 is a no-brainer.
- Multi-location operators (3+ cities) have budget and pain — they're the $2,497 tier.

### Why NOT to compete on price
- A $99/mo website guy is competing with Squarespace and Wix. You'll lose.
- A $497/mo website guy who books jobs is competing with a marketing agency. You'll win because you're cheaper AND more specialized.

### The "what if they say no?" backup
If $497 feels too steep, offer:
- $497 setup + $297/mo (locks them in for 6 months).
- $997/quarter paid upfront (cash flow for you, 17% discount for them).

### Free / pilot offer
For your first 3 clients, do it at 50% off in exchange for:
- A case study (with their permission)
- A video testimonial
- Permission to show their site as a portfolio piece

These first 3 clients pay for themselves in referrals.

## 3. Outreach — getting your first 5 clients

The trades niche is gold because **owners are approachable** and **decision-making is fast**. One owner decides. No procurement department.

### Channel 1: In-person (highest conversion)
Walk into 5–10 trade businesses near you per week. Bring a tablet showing the live demo.

Script:
> "Hey, I'm [name] — I build AI websites and 24/7 receptionist agents for [trade] companies around here. Got a minute? I want to show you something — my client calls this at 2am and gets a lead that would've gone to your competitor."

**Conversion rate:** 10–25% of warm intros. Best ROI of any channel.

### Channel 2: Cold email
Target: trade businesses with bad websites (slow, no mobile, no chat).

Where to find them: Google "[trade] + [your city]" → look at sites ranking 1–10. If they're bad, that's a prospect.

Template:
```
Subject: Your [trade] website is losing you jobs

Hi [first name],

I'm a local AI websites guy — I noticed your site takes 8 seconds to load
and has no way to capture leads after hours.

I built a free AI receptionist demo that handles after-hours leads. Here's
a 60-second video of it working: [link]

If you want me to look at your site and tell you exactly what I'd fix,
reply "audit" and I'll send a free report.

— [Your name]
[phone]
```

**Open rate:** 30–40% (specific subject)
**Reply rate:** 5–15%
**Conversion to call:** 30–50% of replies
**Conversion to client:** 30% of calls

So 100 emails → ~10 calls → ~3 clients. At $997/mo, that's $36K/yr recurring.

### Channel 3: Google Business Profile
Set up YOUR business as "AI websites for [trade]" on Google Business Profile. Optimize it. Many of your clients will find YOU.

### Channel 4: Facebook groups
Join "[Your City] Home Services", "[Your City] Plumbers", etc. Don't spam — answer questions, be useful. When someone asks for a website recommendation, you're there.

### Channel 5: Referrals (after month 3)
Every happy client = 2–5 referrals over the next year. Ask for them. Specifically: "Do you know 2 other [trade] owners who'd want this?"

## 4. Sales process

Walkthrough (free, 30 min):
1. Ask about their business: how many trucks, revenue range, biggest pain.
2. Look at their current website together. Note 3 specific things wrong.
3. Show them the live demo (`/demo` on YOUR site, configured for their trade).
4. Tell them what you'd build. Specific, not vague.
5. Quote monthly. Discuss 1-pay-it-off-in-90-days outcome.
6. Don't push. Say "I'll send a proposal by tomorrow."

Proposal (sent within 24h):
- 1 paragraph: what I heard about your business
- Bullet list: specific things I'd build
- Pricing (the tier they need)
- 3 references / case studies (or your own before/after)
- "If you want to move forward, just reply 'yes'."

Close rate from proposal: 40–60%.

## 6. Agreement template (one-pager)

> **Services Agreement**
>
> Between [Your Business Name] ("Provider") and [Client Name] ("Client").
>
> **Services.** Provider will build and maintain:
> - A website at [clientdomain.com]
> - A 24/7 AI receptionist trained on Client's services
> - [list specific deliverables for the tier]
>
> **Monthly fee.** $[amount], billed on the [date] of each month. First month due on signing.
>
> **Term.** Month-to-month. Either party may cancel with 30 days' written notice. No refunds for partial months.
>
> **Client responsibilities.** Provide logo, brand colors, photos, and answers to onboarding questions within 7 days of signing. Failure to do so may delay launch.
>
> **Provider responsibilities.** Maintain 99%+ uptime. Notify Client of any planned maintenance 48 hours in advance. Make minor content edits (up to 30 minutes/month) at no charge.
>
> **Ownership.** Upon termination and full payment of all fees, Client owns their website content. Provider retains rights to underlying code and frameworks.
>
> **Liability.** Provider's total liability is limited to fees paid in the prior 3 months.
>
> Signed: _______________ Date: _______________

(Adapt for your state. Don't skip a real lawyer once you're at $10K/mo.)

## 7. When to hire your first contractor

You're a one-person agency until you're at ~$8–10K/mo recurring. Then hire:
- A part-time VA to handle intake calls ($15–20/hr)
- Eventually, a junior developer to build sites ($30–50/hr)
- A salesperson once you have 15+ clients and can train them

Don't hire before the revenue covers them with 50% buffer.
